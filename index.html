Lane Detection System

2023

Inspired by Tesla's autopilot system during a trip to the USA, I developed a real-time lane detection system using OpenCV and Python. The project applies computer vision techniques like lane isolation, perspective transform, binary image transform, and line fitting to identify road lanes under varying conditions. I've also implemented curvature calculation and center-of-road line adjustment. Currently working on integrating vehicle detection and relative speed calculations to further enhance its capabilities.

OpenCV Python Computer Vision Autonomous Driving

FPGA Rhythm Game

2024

As part of my EE2026 Digital Design course, our team built a rhythm game using a Basys3 FPGA board with PMOD OLED, DA2, and AMP2 modules. I handled the audio system, applying my musical background to generate sound from clock cycles. By mapping guitar notes to specific frequencies and durations, I created a system for playing 5 different songs with volume control. My teammate and I took lead responsibility for integrating all components into a cohesive game that synchronizes audio with on-screen visuals, all built within a tight two-week timeframe.

Verilog FPGA Digital Design Audio Processing

Augmented Reality Video Player

2024

Fascinated by AR headsets but deterred by their high cost, I created my own AR video player using computer vision techniques. Starting with hand tracking and object tracking, I built a system that allows me to move digital objects with hand gestures. I then developed it into a video player with a custom GUI that can be resized and repositioned in space. I implemented a clever mode-switching system to distinguish between resizing and moving gestures, solving a key usability challenge. The final product closely mimics the interactive experience of commercial AR headsets.

Computer Vision Python Augmented Reality Hand Tracking
